You are the **Frozen Decoder** of a Video-OPD potential-space reasoning system.

Important: you are not an independently-running model with your own conversation. You are a **frozen copy of the student's initial LM head** (a single linear layer `hidden_dim → vocab_size`). At every latent step the student produces a hidden state; you project that hidden state into vocabulary logits so that humans (and the training loss) can read out what the student is "saying" in latent space.

Your behavioural contract (this is what training enforces, via either CE-to-GT in SFT or KL-to-Teacher_R/Teacher_P in OPD)
1. When you decode an **odd latent block** (reasoning), the readout text must look like a reasoning span that ends either with
   - `... <observe type="..." target="..." .../>` — when the block ends with a tool call, **the very last characters MUST be `/>`**, signalling the state machine to enter the next (even) perception block; or
   - `[Conclude] ...` (or `[Reason] ...`) **with no trailing `/>`** — signalling the state machine to stop and emit `</think>`.
2. When you decode an **even latent block** (perception), the readout text must be a single short perception sentence (no `<result>` tags), matching what Teacher_P would have produced for the preceding observe call.
3. Each block's readout must terminate at an end-of-block (EOB) signal — by default, the EOS token or a newline `\n` — within at most 128 tokens. The training data and Teacher_R/Teacher_P generations all respect this 128-token-per-block cap.
4. You never modify hidden states, you never participate in gradient updates, you only project hidden → vocab. The whole point of keeping you frozen is to anchor the student's latent space to a stable language-space coordinate system so:
   - in SFT, the student's hidden states can be supervised by GT text via a normal CE loss on your projected logits;
   - in OPD, the student's hidden states can be supervised by Teacher_R / Teacher_P logits via KL on your projected logits;
   - at inference time, your projected `argmax` token sequence per block tells the state machine whether the next block should be an observe (perception) block or a `</think>` (finish) signal.

Implementation note (informational only)
- Concretely you live as a `torch.nn.Linear(hidden_dim, vocab_size, bias=False)` whose weights are deep-copied from `student.lm_head` at the start of training and then `requires_grad_(False)`.
- This text file is loaded at training-script start-up purely as documentation; nothing here changes your numerical behaviour. It exists so a human reader can understand which role this frozen module plays in the architecture.
